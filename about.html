    Time for change

    Banks ridden with heavy overheads dictate the terms and conditions for both borrowers and investors (savers) to sustain themselves and report high margins, all these to the detriment of the business community. The small businesses have been relegated to the periphery with no access to the much needed finance and support for growth. Commercial banking as we know it today will undergo disruption. There are clear trends that point to this direction; first, the technological explosion and growth of mobile money has already started changing commercial banking as was witnessed in Kenya with emergence of Mpesa. There are a lot more people who no longer see the need to visit a bank branch as long as their Mpesa is loaded. The second trend is the growth of big data and superior analytics that render the manual credit appraisal irrelevant. Thirdly, the change in demography with emergence of millennials who are no longer familiar with traditional commercial banks points to this disruption.
